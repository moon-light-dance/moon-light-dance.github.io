Duel

PRESENTATION!

2 long years! It took a lot of blood, sweat, and tears. But after a long, LONG time, I finished this project.

Theres a lot to say- and yet words fail to reach my fingers. I'll update this as time goes on, because I'm bound to miss a few details if I try to recount everything right now.

Duel was the 3rd "real" animation I've worked on. By real, I mean more than a couple seconds. I was dissatisfied with my first (and longest) real animation, so I scrapped it. The 2nd animation was the "Gabriel calls me cringe" animation. During the 1-month production of that project, I was filled with so many hopes and ideas about the future. The entire time, I was thinking "I really hope this gets at least around 10-thousand views. I can be boosted at least a hundred subs, and any videos I make after the fact will be seen by more people!" And then, that October, I uploaded it, and everything I had hoped for came true. I was overjoyed!

Music is essential to how I work. Without music, I cant function as an animator. As I doodled little Gabriels, I listened to the Ultrakill Soundtrack. One day, while "Duel" was playing, I decided to pay more attention to the song. Then I was like "Hey, this song RIPS! I love this!" And at that moment I felt MOTIVATED! This song was AWESOME! Fast-forward a couple months. I'm sitting in my 3-D art class, and my mind drifts back to Duel. I start imagining these sick shots and sweeping camera movements. Then I made a decision. "Ideally the whole project should take 4 months max, but one thing is for sure: I'm done letting this stay in my head. I am going to work on Duel, and I am going to FINISH it, no matter how long it takes. After all, the Gabriel animation succeded! If I shoot higher, that could work too!" That was February of 2023. It did not take 4 months. But, during the first half of those 2 years, I would recall that moment where Duel "clicked" with me, where I felt motivated. And here we are now. You might ask me: "Why spend so much effort and time?" Here's my answer: "I liked the music."

Part of the reason Duel took so long to make was because I held myself to such high standards. Take for example this shot, which is only held on for 2 frames. I constantly re-iterated on the same frames multiple times until I was satisfied with the result. I went through all 5500 frames of that animation, redrawing, repositioning, and refining, multiple times. Not to mention all the cut content.

But enough of me yapping. Lets see some art!
